There’s an overcast sky above. I thank the non-existent God above for that. It would have been hot as hell otherwise. As soon as the thought enters my mind, the sky opens up and drops an atomic bomb of water. Well, this figures. Thanks God. The rain keeps coming down, and I keep walking. My clothes are soaked. It’s funny though because I don’t really care. I feel like I just escaped death back there. Grim death is always right behind me, but he was at my throat back there in that shithole tavern. I’m not sure why it scared me so much. I had just tried to end it all a couple days ago. What has changed between then and now. Have I changed? Do people change? Cars drive past and muddy water sprays up at me. One car pulls over. I can tell it’s a woman and a young boy in the car. It’s Lillian. “Do you want a ride?” she asks. I climb into the backseat, and she pulls off.
